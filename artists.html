Don Pablo were born out of the whimsy that followed the beginning of a new millennium. Neither in or out of fashion their purple patch, between 2002 and 2006, produced a collection of wonderfully imaginative songs.

Now long since buried beneath the weight of responsibility and service delivery the duo no longer have the capacity to create but 8 Meg Stick Records are committed to unearthing the treasure trove that is Don Pablo’s output and making it available to literally anyone that will listen.

Their first single Campfire is the internal monologue of an overwhelming affair that will ultimately draw to an unsatisfactory close. Hammond organ and a hypnotic melody on the bass frame the vocals until all is given way to drums and anger before returning to the resign of the bass as the track draws it’s last moment…

We Used To See The Sky, an indie/alternative rock band from Bristol UK, owe their existence to Damon Albarn.

When singer/guitarist Mike Grant and keyboardist/producer Paul Wheatcroft decided to book a studio session together, it was only meant to be a side project from the Damon Albarn tribute act they were rehearsing at the time.

There were no expectations about what would come out of the evening, but by the end of it they had a complete original track recorded, and both of them soon decided that writing their own music was much more fun than playing someone else’s. Lead guitarist Steve Collard came on board shortly thereafter, and with a bucketload of ideas and track stems flying around, the band decided to take their name from the title of one of their first songs. New drummer Steve Sansum joined in December 2018.
